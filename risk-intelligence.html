Why It Matters

Risks often appear before the final review.

Many business risks are not created at the final legal or compliance review stage. They appear earlier — in product descriptions, sales promises, marketing language, supplier files, operating procedures, and delivery documents.

Product & Market Risk
Product specifications, certifications, and market requirements may not match the target region or customer scenario.
Sales & Marketing Risk
Sales materials or promotional language may include overstatements, unclear commitments, or compliance-sensitive expressions.
Contract & Document Risk
Contracts, letters, customer responses, and legal documents may contain unclear responsibilities, payment risks, data security issues, or outdated clauses.
Operational Process Risk
Procurement, supplier management, after-sales service, and cross-region delivery may miss key approval steps, required materials, or updated procedures.
How It Works

From enterprise knowledge to risk-aware action

A six-step process that connects your business content to the governed knowledge in Knowledge Hub — surfacing risks before they become problems.

01
Business Content Input
Users submit or draft business content — product descriptions, sales materials, customer documents, supplier files, contracts, or operational records.
02
Knowledge Hub Matching
The system checks the content against governed knowledge — product standards, policies, regulations, contract templates, historical issues, and expert experience.
03
Scenario Recognition
The system identifies whether the content relates to production, sales, marketing, delivery, contracts, compliance, or operations.
04
Risk Detection
Potential risks are flagged — outdated versions, missing materials, over-promising, unclear responsibilities, data security concerns, or regional requirement mismatches.
05
Source Tracing & Explanation
Each risk links back to supporting sources — policy documents, product standards, regulatory references, historical cases, approval records, or expert knowledge.
06
Suggested Next Action
The system suggests next steps — revising wording, updating a document version, adding required materials, triggering approval, or escalating to legal/compliance review.
Typical Use Scenarios

Risk intelligence across business workflows

Risk Intelligence is not limited to contracts or compliance. It applies wherever your teams produce business content that could create risks if not properly reviewed against enterprise knowledge.

Scenario 01
Global Manufacturing
A global manufacturer prepares product materials for different regional markets. Risk Intelligence checks product descriptions, certification requirements, warranty language, and market-specific rules — helping teams identify potential compliance gaps before customer communication.
Scenario 02
Sales & Customer Commitments
Sales teams prepare proposals, quotations, or customer responses. The system flags unclear commitments, unsupported claims, delivery risks, or terms that may exceed internal policy.
Scenario 03
Marketing & Public Communication
Marketing teams draft external content. Risk Intelligence identifies overstatements, sensitive wording, regulatory risks, or expressions that may not align with internal standards or local requirements.
Scenario 04
Contracts & Legal Documents
Business teams prepare contracts, letters, statements, or customer-facing documents. The system checks responsibility boundaries, payment terms, liability clauses, data security requirements, and outdated template usage.
Capability Design

More than rule checking. Connected risk intelligence.

Risk Intelligence is designed around your enterprise's own knowledge — not generic rule libraries. Here is what that means in practice.

Powered by enterprise-specific knowledge
Risk checks are grounded in the company's own standards, policies, procedures, templates, and historical experience — not generic rule libraries or public databases.
Context-aware, not keyword-only
The system understands business context, scenario type, and the relationship between content, rules, and risk signals — rather than matching keywords against a fixed checklist.
Source-traceable risk explanation
Every risk alert explains why it matters and where the supporting knowledge comes from — giving teams the confidence to act rather than just a warning flag.
Closed-loop risk learning
Reviewed risks, corrections, and approval outcomes can flow back into Knowledge Hub — making future risk detection more accurate and making the enterprise knowledge base more complete over time.
