Why It Matters

Why sales contracts and commercial documents need risk review

Commercial documents directly affect payment, delivery, liability, and customer commitments. Each clause carries business consequence. These are the four areas where issues most often appear.

Payment & Commercial Terms

Payment milestones, credit terms, pricing, and discount structures must match company financial policy. A non-standard payment period or off-policy discount can affect cash flow, approval routing, and recoverability.

Common risk Payment period or milestone does not match the latest payment policy.

Delivery & Service Commitments

Delivery timelines, service-level commitments, and implementation windows must reflect actual delivery capability. Over-promised timelines lead to missed milestones, customer disputes, and after-sales liabilities.

Common risk Promised delivery timeline is shorter than the standard implementation cycle.

Liability, IP & Confidentiality

Liability caps, IP ownership, data protection, and confidentiality clauses must align with approved legal language. Unbalanced or missing clauses expose the company to financial, legal, and compliance risk.

Common risk Liability clause is uncapped or IP / confidentiality terms are incomplete.

Template Consistency & Approval Rules

Contracts must be built on the latest approved template, follow the correct approval workflow, and respect customer-specific risk notes. An outdated template or bypassed approval creates version drift and audit gaps.

Common risk Document is based on an outdated contract template or skips required review.
A Commercial Document Review Scenario

Sales Contract & Business Document Review

Help sales and commercial teams identify risks before contracts and business commitments move forward.

The story

A sales manager is preparing a contract and commercial proposal for a key customer. To move the deal forward quickly, the document includes payment terms, delivery commitments, service scope, warranty statements, liability clauses, and customer-specific requirements. Before sending it for approval or signature, Business Content Intelligence reviews the document against the company's Knowledge Hub and highlights potential risks.

Who is using it

Sales / Commercial Manager, supported by Legal, Finance, Delivery, and Management teams.

What they are working on

Sales contracts, quotations, SOWs, service commitments, commercial proposals, and business terms.
