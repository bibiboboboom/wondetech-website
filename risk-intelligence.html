Why It Matters

Risks are created in daily business actions
— not only in final review.

Most compliance problems do not start at the legal review desk. They begin when someone writes a product description, promises a delivery date, publishes marketing copy, or signs off on an operating procedure.

Product Claims & Specifications

A product description may sound correct internally, but it may not match the certification, warranty, or market requirement of the target region.

Example: "Industry-leading durability" appears in a datasheet for a market where specific ISO test data is required by regulation.
Sales Promises & Commitments

A proposal may include delivery promises, service commitments, or performance claims that exceed internal policy or actual capability.

Example: A sales proposal promises "30-day delivery worldwide," but internal logistics policy allows only regional SLAs with longer lead times for certain zones.
Marketing & Public Communication

Promotional content may use overstatements, absolute language, or region-sensitive claims that create compliance or reputational risk.

Example: A press release uses "best-in-class globally" without substantiating data, which could trigger advertising-standards scrutiny in some markets.
Contracts, Supplier Files & SOPs

A contract, SOP, or supplier file may use an outdated template, miss required approvals, or leave responsibility boundaries unclear.

Example: An updated supplier agreement reuses a clause from a previous version that no longer reflects current data-protection requirements.

Capability Design

Why it is different from simple rule checking

Risk Intelligence is designed around how enterprises actually work — using your own knowledge, understanding context, and learning over time.

Enterprise-specific knowledge grounding

Risk alerts are based on the company's own standards, policies, procedures, templates, and historical experience — not generic rule libraries or public databases.

Context-aware analysis

The system considers business scenario, document type, region, product context, and user intent — instead of matching keywords against a fixed checklist.

Source-traceable explanations

Each alert shows why the risk matters and where the supporting knowledge comes from — giving teams confidence to act rather than just receiving a warning flag.

Closed-loop risk learning

Corrections, review results, and approval outcomes can return to Knowledge Hub — improving future risk detection and keeping the knowledge base more accurate over time.
