Why It Matters

Business content creates risk every day

Most review issues are not caught by the author. They surface after the document has been sent, signed, or published — when fixing them is slow, expensive, and reputationally costly.

Contracts & Commercial Terms

Payment terms, delivery commitments, liability clauses, and service scope may create financial or legal risks.

Product Documentation

Specifications, safety warnings, certification statements, and warranty terms may not match the latest product or target market.

Marketing Claims

Product claims, certification references, and promotional wording may be unsupported or too broad for the target audience.

SOPs & Operational Files

Outdated procedures, missing safety steps, or inconsistent training materials may create execution risks across sites.

How It Helps

Three things it does for every document

Business Content Intelligence does not replace human review. It accelerates it — by surfacing the questions that reviewers should ask, and the knowledge they should check against.

01

Spot the risk

Identify risky wording, outdated information, missing requirements, or non-standard commitments in the draft.

02

Trace the source

Show the policy, template, standard, clause, historical case, or internal rule behind each finding.

03

Suggest the next action

Recommend safer wording, required review, approval routing, or follow-up action — so the team knows what to do next.
