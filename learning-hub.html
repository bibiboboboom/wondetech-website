Why Learning Hub

Turn enterprise knowledge into workforce capability

Generic training programs do not build real operational readiness. Learning Hub supports targeted development using your own enterprise knowledge as the benchmark — then captures results back into that knowledge base.

Role-Based Competency Maps

Every role gets a structured competency map derived from your actual job requirements and operational standards. Not generic templates — your enterprise-specific requirements.

Competency Gap Analysis

Learning Hub identifies exactly where each employee falls short relative to their role requirements — and prioritizes what to address first based on criticality.

EDP-Based Development

Each employee receives an Employee Development Plan based on their specific gaps — not a generic course catalog. Learning is targeted, measurable, and tied to role readiness.

Manager Dashboards

Team leads and managers see role readiness scores, completion progress, and critical gaps across their workforce — with full audit trails for compliance.

Always Synchronized

When a procedure or standard updates in Knowledge Hub, related learning content and competency maps update automatically. Your training stays aligned with current operations.

Completion & Certification Tracking

Track who has completed what, when, and to what standard. Generate compliance records and certification reports for HSE and regulatory requirements.

Multilingual Live Training

Train across languages — built for international oilfield teams

International oilfield projects often involve trainers and learners speaking different languages. Learning Hub supports live training sessions where English and Arabic speakers can follow along in their preferred language — reducing communication barriers during remote or hybrid instruction.

English–Arabic Bilingual Support

Training sessions can be delivered and followed in both English and Arabic. Employees receive materials and session context in their working language, without waiting for manual translation after the event.

Industry-Specific Training Context

Unlike general-purpose meeting tools, Learning Hub is designed for oilfield training scenarios — safety drills, equipment operation, field procedures — where precise terminology and structured instruction matter.

Post-Session Knowledge Capture

Session transcripts are captured and translated into a standard searchable format. Q&A exchanges and key explanations are indexed and linked back to the relevant competency areas in Knowledge Hub.

Designed for Distributed Oilfield Teams

Remote sessions, hybrid training, and cross-site instruction are common in international oil and gas operations. Learning Hub is better suited to these industry-specific training needs than a generic meeting platform.

How It Works

From knowledge base to measurable role readiness

Step 1

Knowledge Foundation

Learning Hub reads from Knowledge Hub — operational standards, procedures, expert transcripts, training outputs, and field records for each role.

↓
Step 2

Competency Mapping

Each role is mapped to the enterprise-specific knowledge and skills it requires. Maps are derived from your own documentation — not generic frameworks.

↓
Step 3

Gap Identification

Each employee's knowledge state is assessed against their role map. Gaps are scored by severity so the most critical needs surface first.

↓
Step 4

EDP-Based Development

Employees receive targeted learning assignments tied to their specific gaps. Content comes directly from the knowledge base — including live training outputs.

↓
Step 5

Verified Readiness

Role readiness scores update as employees complete learning. Training results and feedback loop back into Knowledge Hub — enriching it for future use.

Online & Offline Training

From digital learning to field practice — one continuous workflow

Oilfield training cannot be fully moved online. Safety drills, firefighting practice, equipment operation, and field procedures require offline instruction and hands-on practice. Learning Hub connects every stage into a single, traceable training workflow.

Online Course Scheduling

Role-based online learning paths are scheduled and tracked within the platform. Employees know what to complete before attending offline or field training sessions.

Classroom & Field Training Records

Offline sessions — classroom instruction, site-based drills, and equipment walkthroughs — are registered in the platform. Attendance, completion status, and outcomes are recorded alongside online activity.

Assessment & Practical Evaluation

Written assessments for online modules and practical evaluations for hands-on training are recorded in a unified record. Managers can see both theoretical knowledge scores and field-verified competency.

Instructor Feedback & Debrief

Instructors and field supervisors add qualitative feedback directly in the platform — noting specific gaps observed during drills or site operations. This feeds back into each employee's development plan.

Mohammed's Development Journey

From new joiner confusion to field readiness — how Learning Hub supports every stage of employee growth.

Step 1 of 6

Mohammed joins as a new field engineer — surrounded by manuals, procedures, and technical documents, with no clear starting point.

Step 2 of 6

Learning Hub generates a role-based development plan from his JD and competency model — giving Mohammed a clear, structured path forward.

Step 3 of 6

He completes bilingual courses, watches live training sessions, and checks off tasks — all tracked automatically in the system.

Step 4 of 6

Mohammed applies his learning on-site, working alongside experienced engineers. Practical exposure and collaboration reinforce classroom knowledge.

Step 5 of 6

His manager reviews progress data, completed modules, and skills growth. Development actions are refined based on real learning signals and feedback.

Step 6 of 6

Mohammed reaches role readiness — contributing confidently in the field, mentoring new joiners, and continuing his development cycle.
